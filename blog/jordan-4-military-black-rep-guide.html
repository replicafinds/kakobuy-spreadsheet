The Jordan 4 Military Black is one of the most popular colorways in the rep market right now. High demand means lots of sellers — and lots of variance in quality. This guide covers exactly what to look for and where to find a seller who gets it right.

What Makes the Military Black Difficult

The Jordan 4 Military Black uses a specific combination of materials: the tumbled leather upper, the icy blue translucent sole, and the khaki inner lining. Each of these has to be accurate for the shoe to look right, and they come from different parts of the production process.

The sole unit is the most common failure point. The correct sole has a specific curvature — it should arc upward toward the toe when placed flat, not sit completely flat. The translucency of the icy sole should be visible in natural light. Lower-quality batches use a more opaque sole that looks slightly wrong, especially when compared side by side.

The Leather Upper

The tumbled leather texture should have a consistent grain pattern — not too smooth and not exaggerated. The heel tab stitching should be clean and the Jumpman logo on the tongue should be correctly sized and positioned. The lace tips should be metal or close to it, not cheap plastic.

Ask for QC photos of the sole unit from below, the heel tab, and the tongue label. These three photos tell you almost everything about the batch quality.

Sizing

Jordan 4s run true to size for most buyers. Go true to size unless you have a wide foot, in which case going half a size up is common. Sole flex and toe box shape are easier to assess from QC photos than from standard product shots.
