How to Consolidate a KakoBuy Haul — Save on Shipping Every Time

Consolidation is the single most important thing beginners miss when using KakoBuy. Get this right and you can save $30-60 per haul compared to shipping items individually. Here is how it works.

How KakoBuy Shipping Works

KakoBuy is a purchasing agent — they buy items from Chinese sellers, warehouse them, and forward them to you. The key cost structure is: **shipping is charged per package, not per item**. A single consolidated package of ten items costs roughly the same to ship as a package of two items.

This means the more you order in a single batch, the lower your per-item shipping cost. A haul of eight pieces shipped together might cost $35 total. The same eight pieces shipped individually would cost significantly more.

The Warehousing Window

Once KakoBuy receives an item from the seller, it sits in their warehouse at no cost for a set period (typically 90 days). This gives you a window to accumulate items before shipping.

The optimal approach: spend 3-4 weeks adding items to your warehouse, then consolidate and ship everything at once. If you find yourself wanting to ship after adding just one or two items, add them to a pending list and wait until you have at least five or six pieces.

QC Photos Before Consolidation

Before you approve consolidation, request QC photos for every single item. This is not optional — it is how you catch problems before they become your problem. KakoBuy will photograph each piece and send you the images. Review each one carefully:

- Shoes: sole unit, toe box, tongue label, heel tab - Hoodies: badge or logo, fabric, stitching - Jackets: hardware, patches, fill loft - Accessories: hardware details, material quality

If anything looks wrong, reject the item before consolidation. KakoBuy can hold the rejected item while you source a replacement from a different seller.

Shipping Route Selection

KakoBuy offers several shipping routes at different price points and speeds. The main tradeoffs are cost vs. speed vs. customs risk. For most buyers shipping to Europe or North America, the mid-tier routes (not the cheapest, not the premium express) offer the best balance.
