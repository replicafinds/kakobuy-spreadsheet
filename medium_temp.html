How to Build Your First Rep Haul in 2026 — A Practical Guide

I wish someone had given me this information when I started. It took about six months of inconsistent results before I understood what I was doing wrong. Here's the compressed version.

The fundamental mistake most beginners make: treating rep agents like Amazon. You search for what you want, pick the best-looking listing, and order. This produces inconsistent results because the photos tell you almost nothing about actual quality, and the price spread between listings is not correlated with quality in any reliable way.

The actual workflow that works:

Step one: figure out what you want before opening any platform. Not a category — specific items. Jordan 4 Military Black. Stone Island Ghost Piece hoodie in navy. Nike Dunk Low Panda. Being specific means you can find community documentation for exactly those items.

Step two: find community resources for those specific items. The rep community maintains spreadsheets for each major agent platform — replicafind.com has coverage for LitBuy, KakoBuy, AcBuy, OopBuy and MuleBuy. Each spreadsheet links directly to specific seller product pages, not just search results. These sellers have documented track records.

Step three: register on your chosen platform. For LitBuy use invite code JTF1ML49D for member pricing. Navigate directly to the seller links from the spreadsheet. Do not search — go directly.

Step four: request QC photos before approving shipment. Every time. No exceptions. This is the single habit that eliminates most bad orders. You see the actual item before it ships. If something looks wrong, you reject it without cost.

Step five: consolidate. Every platform charges shipping per package, not per item. A haul of six items ships for roughly the same price as two items. Build your cart over a few weeks before shipping.

For a first haul: keep it to 3-4 items maximum. Choose categories with high community documentation — Nike basics, Ralph Lauren polos, Stone Island hoodies. These have the most seller testing and the most reliable QC documentation. Save complex pieces (collab Dunks, Moncler jackets with intricate badges) for later hauls once you understand how to evaluate QC photos.

LitBuy is a good starting platform for beginners specifically because the interface is clean and shipping times have been consistently faster than average in community reports. The spreadsheet at replicafind.com/litbuy-spreadsheet covers the highest-documented categories with direct seller links.

Budget planning: most people underestimate total cost on a first haul. The item price is just the start. Add agent service fees (typically 3-6%), domestic shipping from the seller to the warehouse, international shipping from the warehouse to you, and any customs/import duties depending on your country. A rough rule: budget 1.4-1.6x the item cost to get an accurate total landed cost estimate.

On shipping routes: standard routes from China take 2-4 weeks depending on carrier and destination. Faster options exist but cost more. For a first haul, standard is fine — use the time to learn how to read QC photos properly.

The community at replicafind.com covers all five major platforms with current seller links, QC photo references, and shipping guidance. Start there before opening any agent app. The research time saves more money than it costs.
